Research

My research is in commutative algebra and algebraic combinatorics.
    On the commutative algebra side, my interests include questions about depth, multiplicities and measures of singularity, Cohen-Macaulay representation theory, and prime characteristic methods. I have been working recently on the ideal case of the Huneke-Wiegand conjecture and some related questions.
    In algebraic combinatorics, I am interested in Stanley-Reisner theory and understanding certain homological conditions, such as bounds on depth or Serre conditions, from the simplicial complex perspective. I have been working recently on studying matroid complexes via polarizations of their Stanley-Reisner ideals as well as some continuations of my previous work on higher nerve complexes.
